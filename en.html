Stone is a language
written by time

For over three decades, our family workshop has carried forward the ancient art of Chinese stone carving — a tradition stretching back through the Yungang Grottoes and the master carvers of Quyang. We bridge millennia, bringing timeless technique into dialogue with contemporary aesthetics.

Our stones come from China's most celebrated quarries. Each piece passes through a master's hands for months — rough shaping, fine detailing, patient polishing — until the spirit within the stone reveals itself. These are not mere objects. They are heirlooms.

Three Generations
of Stone Mastery

Our story began in the stone villages of Quyang, where my grandfather first placed a chisel in my father's hands. Three decades later, we have journeyed through Qingtian, Hui'an, and Shoushan — learning from each region's masters, absorbing their secrets, and forging a style uniquely our own.

"Every stone has its own grain and temperament. The master's calling is not to conquer it, but to listen — to find the form that was always waiting inside. Each chisel strike is an act of reverence."

The Craft

From Raw Stone to Living Art

I

Selection

We visit quarries in person — reading the grain, testing the density, listening to the resonance of each stone. Only the finest earn a place in our workshop.

II

Design

Guided by the stone's natural form and veining, we sketch designs that honor the material. Each concept is refined until stone and vision become one.

III

Carving

Bold strokes establish the form; patient chiseling reveals the spirit. From rough block to fine detail, the process spans months — sometimes years.

IV

Finishing

Layer upon layer of polishing — from coarse grit to silk-smooth. A final wax seal reveals a lustrous warmth that deepens with the passage of time.

Precious Stones

Quarried from China's Finest Sources

White Marble

汉白玉

Beijing Fangshan · Sichuan Baoxing

Pure white with a warm, jade-like translucency. The stone of emperors and temples — prized for Buddhist sculpture and ceremonial architecture for over a millennium. Remarkably resistant to weathering and discoloration.

Imperial Stone Luminous Weather-Resistant

Bluestone

青石

Shandong Jiaxiang · Fujian Hui'an

Dense, dignified grey-blue with a centuries-old pedigree. China's most traditional carving stone — ideal for large architectural pieces, garden sculptures, and ceremonial works with an air of quiet grandeur.

Dense & Sturdy Time-Honored Versatile

Granite

花岗岩

Fujian Quanzhou · Shandong Rizhao

Exceptionally hard with a rich spectrum — from sesame white to charcoal black and warm amber. Unmatched in durability for outdoor monuments and architectural elements that must endure for generations.

Rich Palette Extreme Hardness Outdoor-Grade
